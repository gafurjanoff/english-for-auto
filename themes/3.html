TASK 3 Read the tips to write an influential resume.
Tips to write a good resume
  • Write a summary that will keep people reading. The summary should describe your occupation, your qualifications, and perhaps the type of job you are looking for, if that is not immediately obvious.
  • Make your employment history about your most impressive accomplishments on the job. List the relevant jobs you have held in your field for the past 10 to 15 years, providing the name and location of the company, your job title, and the years you worked there.
  • Craft your education section according to its importance in your career path. List your educational achievements also in reverse chronological order, with the highest degree first.
  • Highlight your most impressive talents in your skills list. Include a list of your most marketable hard and soft skills.

TASK 5 “MECHANIC SKILLS WORKSHOP”

Objective: To improve professional qualities like communication, problem-solving, time management, and customer service while reinforcing technical knowledge.

Time: 2–3 hours

Step 1: Diagnosis Role-Play (45 minutes)

Objective: Practice problem-solving, active listening, and customer communication.

  1. Preparation: Assign two roles – Mechanic and Customer.
  2. Scenario Setup: Give the “Customer” a scenario card describing a car issue (e.g., “The car makes a rattling noise above 40 mph” or “The brakes feel less responsive than usual”).
  3. Role-Play: The “Mechanic” listens to the “Customer’s” description, asks questions to narrow down the issue, and explains potential causes in simple terms.
  4. Feedback: After each round, provide feedback on the mechanic's communication skills, including their tone, clarity, and empathy toward the customer’s concern.
Step 2: Hands-On Team Challenge (1 hour)

Objective: Improve teamwork, time management, and technical skills.

  1. Divide the mechanics into small teams and assign a specific task, like “Diagnose and fix a starter issue” or “Complete a brake pad replacement.”
  2. Set a Time Limit: Allow 20 minutes for diagnosis and 40 minutes for repair.
  3. Encourage Delegation: Each team should assign specific roles (e.g., lead mechanic, parts manager, quality checker) to simulate a shop environment.
  4. Debrief: Afterward, discuss what went well, areas for improvement, and any time-saving techniques used.
Step 3: Customer Communication Debrief (30 minutes)

Objective: Enhance customer service skills and gain feedback on approach.

  1. Scenario-Based Feedback: Each team presents their findings to the “Customer” (could be an instructor or peer).
  2. Emphasis on Clarity: The team should communicate the issue, repair performed, and any preventative maintenance tips in clear, simple language.
  3. Group Discussion: Review as a group how to manage customer expectations and communicate delays.
