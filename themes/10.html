LESSON 10. Automated systems

TASK 1 Read the text below and write short definitions for each **bolded** term.

Example of automated manufacturing system

Automated systems are technologies or processes designed to operate independently with minimal human intervention. They utilize software, machinery, and algorithms to perform tasks such as data processing, manufacturing, and control functions. These systems enhance efficiency, accuracy, and speed by automating repetitive or complex tasks.

Define the following terms in your own words (1–2 sentences each):

  1. operate independently
  2. minimal human intervention
  3. software
  4. data processing
  5. manufacturing
  6. control functions
  7. efficiency
  8. accuracy
  9. repetitive tasks
  10. complex tasks
Example definitions:

1. operate independently — work without needing constant control or input from a person
2. minimal human intervention — very little or no involvement of people during operation
3. software — computer programs and instructions that tell machines what to do
4. data processing — collecting, analyzing, and transforming data into useful information
5. manufacturing — the process of making products using machines and tools
6. control functions — operations that manage and regulate how a system behaves
7. efficiency — achieving maximum results with minimum time, energy or resources
8. accuracy — being correct, precise and without errors
9. repetitive tasks — actions that are done many times in the same way
10. complex tasks — difficult activities that require many steps or decisions

TASK 4 Listen and answer the questions. Describe some benefits of automated car systems.

Sensors used in automated / autonomous vehicles

  • • How do they enhance safety for drivers and pedestrians?
  • • In what ways might they improve traffic flow and reduce congestion?
Possible / model answers:

Safety: Automated systems use sensors (LIDAR, radar, cameras) to detect obstacles, pedestrians, and other vehicles in real time. They can react much faster than humans and apply emergency braking automatically, reducing accidents caused by distraction, fatigue or slow reaction.

Traffic flow & congestion: Vehicles communicate with each other and infrastructure (V2V, V2I), optimize speed and spacing, avoid sudden braking, choose the most efficient routes, and reduce stop-and-go traffic — leading to smoother flow and less congestion.
