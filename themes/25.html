Lesson 25. Regulations and standards

TASK 1 25. Listen, read the text and write the bolded words under the pictures.
Car regulations and standards ensure that vehicles are safe, secure, and environmentally friendly. These regulations cover a variety of areas, including safety, emissions, and fuel economy. Seat belts must be fitted during manufacturing and assembly, and anchorages must be able to withstand a crash. Cars must pass lane departure warning, crash tests (frontal, side, and rollover) before being sold. Some countries have legal requirements for the minimum safety standards of vehicles.

TASK 3 CAR SAFETY & STANDARDS MATCHING GAME (Classroom Activity)
Materials:
  • Printed cards with words (e.g., Seatbelt, Headlights, Brake System, Tyre Standard, Smoke Emissions)
  • Printed cards with definitions or descriptions
Instructions:
  1. Introduction: Discuss why cars have rules (keep people safe, protect environment).
  2. Matching Game: Match word cards to definitions. Explain importance upon matching.
Discussion:
  • “What would happen if cars didn’t have brake lights?”
  • “Why is it important to reduce car smoke emissions?”
