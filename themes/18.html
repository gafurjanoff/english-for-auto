LESSON 18. 4 and 2 stroke engines

TASK 1 18. Listen to the information and define the bolded words.

The main difference between a two-stroke and four-stroke engine is the number of strokes of the piston required to complete a power cycle: Two-stroke. A two-stroke engine completes a power cycle in one revolution of the crankshaft with two strokes of the piston. Four-stroke. A four-stroke engine completes a power cycle in two revolutions of the crankshaft with four strokes of the piston.

TASK 3 Match the definition of the words.
1. Power cycle a. the two strokes in a four-stroke engine cycle that involve moving the piston to either increase the volume of the cylinder or compress the air-fuel mixture
2. Piston b. a small device that ignites the air-fuel mixture in a car's engine, which powers the vehicle
3. Revolutions c. a mechanical component that converts the pistons' up-and-down motion into rotational motion.
4. Intake compression d. a component that converts the energy from fuel combustion into mechanical energy
5. Crankshaft e. a sliding cylinder or metal disc that moves up and down inside a tube to cause parts of an engine to move
6. Spark plug f. the number of times the engine's crankshaft rotates in one minute.
7. Piston fuel g. the process of turning a device or system off and then on again.
8. Two-stroke h. a type of internal combustion engine that completes a power cycle in just two strokes of the piston
