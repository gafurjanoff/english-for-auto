LESSON 16. Supply system of internal combustion engine

TASK 1 Read the information and define the bolded words.

The supply system of an internal combustion engine (ICE) refers to the mechanisms and components that deliver the essential inputs— fuel and air—needed for combustion, as well as the systems that manage emissions and optimize performance. These systems are crucial for ensuring that the engine operates efficiently, reliably, and in compliance with environmental regulations.

optimize → improve or make more efficient

TASK 3 Listen and complete the gaps.

Working Principle of supply system of internal combustion engine

  1. Air Intake: Clean air enters the system through the air .
  2. Fuel Delivery: The fuel pump draws fuel from the tank and delivers it to the carburetor or injectors.
  3. Mixing: In the carburetor, the fuel is mixed with air, while in modern engines, fuel spray fuel directly into the air intake or combustion chamber for precise mixing.
  4. Distribution: The intake manifold evenly distributes the air-fuel mixture to the cylinders.
  5. Combustion: The mixture is ignited in the cylinders, producing to power the engine.
