LESSON 11. Engines and main mechanisms

TASK 1 Read the information and define the bolded words.

A vehicle is composed of basic mechanical parts and devices making up its systems; each of them fulfills a specific function such as Fuel supply system, Transmission system, Suspension system, Braking system, Steering system, Cooling system, Electrical system, Exhaust system.

TASK 2 11. Listen and fill in gaps.

Cars are complex machines made up of various parts working together to properly. One of the most essential parts is the engine. The engine converts into mechanical power, and it's often referred to as the heart of the car. The engine is responsible for the car and enabling it to move. Another critical is the transmission, which transfers the engine's power to the wheels. The transmission ensures that the car moves at the desired speed by shifting automatically in an automatic transmission, or manually in a manual transmission.

TASK 4
Role Play as a Mechanic

Have the learners work in pairs. One plays the role of a car mechanic, and the other plays a customer whose car has a problem.

  • The "customer" describes a problem with the car using clues. For example, "My car makes a loud noise when I brake."
  • The "mechanic" responds using the vocabulary, e.g., "It sounds like there might be an issue with the braking system."
