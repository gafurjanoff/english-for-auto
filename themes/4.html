TASK 5 Read the interview and make a role play in the classroom.

Interviewer: Good morning, thank you for coming in today. To start, could you please tell me a little about yourself and your experience as a car mechanic?

Mechanic: Good morning, thank you for having me. I’ve been working as an automotive mechanic for over eight years now. I started as an apprentice and gradually gained experience in various areas, including diagnostics, engine repair, suspension, and electrical systems. I’ve worked in both independent shops and larger service centers, which has helped me develop a wide skill set.

Interviewer: Impressive! What tools and equipment are you most familiar with using?

Mechanic: I’m experienced with a range of diagnostic tools, from basic hand tools to more advanced equipment like OBD-II scanners, multimeters, and tire changers. I’ve also worked with computerized diagnostic systems and alignment machines. I'm comfortable working with both manual and automated equipment, depending on the situation.

Interviewer: Great to hear! How do you stay up-to-date with the latest automotive technologies and trends?

Mechanic: I regularly attend workshops and training courses to keep my knowledge current. I’m also subscribed to a few industry magazines and follow online forums and communities. Since automotive technology evolves so rapidly, I make sure I’m always learning about the newest systems, like electric vehicles and hybrid engines.

Interviewer: Sounds like you’re committed to continuous learning, which is essential in this industry.
