LESSON 13. Ignition system of internal combustion engine

TASK 1 Read the information and define the bolded words

Once the ignition switch is prompted by key or the push of a button, it activates the voltage from the battery to the ignition coil to produce the engine spark. The engine spark from the coil or coils is directed to the spark plugs to ignite the fuel to make the vehicle run.

TASK 2 Make a presentation on "Ignition process" using the words below.
Ignition switch, button, activates, voltage, the battery, ignition coil, engine spark, spark plugs, ignite the fuel
Create Your Presentation

Use the words provided above to create a presentation explaining the ignition process. Describe each step in the sequence from when the ignition switch is activated to when the fuel is ignited.

TASK 3 13. Listen to the dialogue and act.
Car Owner: Hi, I'm having trouble starting my car. It just won't turn over. Can you help me figure out what's going on?
Mechanic: Of course! When you say it won't turn over, do you hear the engine trying to start, or is it completely silent when you turn the key?
Car Owner: It makes a clicking sound, but the engine doesn't start. I've had it happen a few times now.
Mechanic: That clicking sound could point to an issue with the ignition system. When was the last time you had your battery checked? A weak battery is one of the most common causes of starting problems.
Car Owner: I replaced the battery about two years ago, but I haven't checked it recently.
Mechanic: Got it. It might still be worth testing the battery just to be sure. If the battery is fine, then the issue could be with the ignition coil or spark plugs. Sometimes, worn spark plugs or a faulty ignition coil can prevent the engine from starting properly.
Car Owner: Ignition coil? I'm not too familiar with that. What does it do?
Mechanic: The ignition coil is responsible for transforming the low voltage from your car's battery into the high voltage needed to create a spark. That spark ignites the air-fuel mixture in the engine, which gets the car running. If the coil is damaged, it won't generate enough voltage to start the engine.
Car Owner: Interesting. So what should we do first?
Mechanic: I'll start by testing your battery and ignition coil to make sure they're working properly. If the battery and coil are fine, we'll inspect the spark plugs. They may need cleaning or replacement. Also, I'll check the ignition switch because, if it's faulty, it might not send the necessary power to the system.
