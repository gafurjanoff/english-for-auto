Plant visits, also known as factory tours, are planned walk-throughs of a manufacturing facility or shop floor. They are usually organized to show customers, suppliers, investors, and other stakeholders an in-depth look at the daily operations of the business.

TASK 3 Perform the activity in the class. Build an Assembly Line
Objective: Participants will simulate an auto factory's assembly line to understand how cars are made, the importance of teamwork, and how efficiency impacts production.
Steps of the Activity:
1. Introductory Discussion (10 minutes)
  • Briefly explain how an auto factory operates.
  • Highlight concepts like assembly lines, specialization of labor, and efficiency.
  • Show a short video or images of real auto factories in action to inspire ideas.
2. Factory Simulation (30 minutes)
Round 1:
  • Participants simulate building a simple car using the materials provided.
  • Measure the time taken to produce a single car.
  • Inspect quality and note errors.
Round 2:
  • Reflect on Round 1. Encourage participants to brainstorm ways to improve the process (e.g., reorganizing the assembly line, redistributing roles).
  • Implement improvements and repeat the production process.
  • Measure time and quality again.
