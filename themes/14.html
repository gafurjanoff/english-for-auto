TASK 1 Listen to the information and define the bolded words.

Cooling circuit and main components

The cooling system works by constantly passing coolant through channels in the engine block. Coolant, driven by a water pump, is pushed through the cylinder block. As the solution passes through these channels, it absorbs heat from the engine. The vehicle's cooling system keeps the engine from burning up from its own heat by pushing coolant that is cooled in the radiator through passages on the engine block and head. The system is somewhat more complex than that, with power transferred from the serpentine belt to the water pump to keep the system moving.

Define the following bolded terms:
  • coolant
  • channels
  • engine block
  • absorbs heat
  • burning up
  • transferred
  • serpentine belt
Model definitions:

coolant → liquid (usually water + antifreeze) that carries heat away from engine
channels → passages/tunnels inside engine block for coolant flow
engine block → main structural part of engine containing cylinders
absorbs heat → takes in/collects heat from surrounding metal
burning up → overheating severely and getting damaged
transferred → moved/passed from one part to another (here: power to pump)
serpentine belt → long belt driving multiple engine accessories including water pump

TASK 2 Answer the questions.

  1. What is the function of coolant?
  2. Name four devices that are important in cooling.
  3. Explain the cooling process step by step.
Model answers:

1. Coolant absorbs heat from the engine and carries it to the radiator to be cooled.

2. Four important devices: water pump, thermostat, radiator, radiator fan (also: hoses, expansion tank, etc.)

3. Step-by-step cooling process:
- Water pump circulates coolant through engine block and head
- Coolant absorbs heat from hot engine parts
- Hot coolant flows to radiator
- Airflow (driving) + fan cools coolant in radiator
- Cooled coolant returns to engine → cycle repeats

TASK 3 Group Discussion on Cooling system in an internal combustion engine

Purpose: Encourage deeper understanding and the use of technical vocabulary.

Instructions: Divide participants into groups (3 people per group). Assign each group one aspect of the cooling system to focus on:

  • Group A: Explain how a water-cooling system works.
  • Group B: Discuss the consequences of a failed cooling system (e.g., overheating, engine damage, performance issues).

Group Task: Each group has 5 minutes to discuss their topic. Then, they prepare a 3-minute explanation to present to the class, answering potential questions like:

  • How does the system function?
  • What components are essential?
  • What are the advantages and disadvantages?

Presentation: After group discussions, each group presents its findings. Other participants are encouraged to ask questions to clarify understanding.
