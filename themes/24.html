Lesson 24. Dimensions and precision

TASK 1 24. Listen and to the information and define the bolded words.
A car's dimensions are its length, width, and height, which are measured from the tips of each dimension. The precision of a car's measurements can affect the quality of the car and the safety of its passengers. The length of a sedan can range from around 15 to 18 feet, with widths between 5 and 6 feet. Heights are typically around 4 to 5 feet. Examples of sedans include the Toyota Camry, Honda Accord, and BMW 3 Series.

TASK 4 Role Play: Alex and Sam.

Alex: Hey Sam! Did you know cars come in all sizes?

Sam: Really? What do you mean?

Alex: Well, every car has dimensions. That means its length, width, and height.

Sam: Oh! So, like how long or tall the car is?

Alex: Exactly! For example, a small car like a compact one might be 4 meters long and 1.8 meters wide.

Sam: And a bigger car, like a truck, would be longer, right?

Alex: Yes! A truck might be 6 meters long or more.

Sam: But why does the size matter?

Alex: Good question! The size helps us know if the car can fit in a garage or parking spot.

Sam: Oh, that’s cool! What about precision? What’s that?

Alex: Precision is like being super careful when measuring. For example, instead of saying the car is 4 meters long, we could say it’s 4.05 meters.

Sam: That’s so exact! Why do we need to be that precise?

Alex: Well, if we’re building a car, even a tiny mistake in size can cause problems.

Sam: I see! So, dimensions and precision help make sure everything is just right!
