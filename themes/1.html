TASK 5
"EXPLORING THE FIRST AUTOMOBILES"

Objective: Develop speaking skills by discussing the history, features, and impact of the first automobiles.

1. Warm-Up Discussion (5-10 minutes)

  • Ask the students: "When do you think the first car was invented?" and "What do you know about early automobiles?"

    Sample: The first practical automobile was invented around 1885-1886 by Karl Benz.

  • Show them images of the first automobiles and encourage them to describe what they see (design, features, differences from modern cars)

    Sample: They had large spoke wheels, no roof, a tiller instead of a steering wheel.
2. Pair/Group Discussion (15-20 minutes)

Prompt Questions:

  • What were some of the challenges early car makers faced?

    Sample: Lack of paved roads, finding fuel, mechanical breakdowns.

  • How did the invention of the automobile change people's lives?

    Sample: Personal freedom of movement, suburbs growth.

  • If you lived in the late 1800s, would you want to ride in one of these first cars? Why or why not?

    Sample: Excited because new technology OR scared because dangerous.

  • How do you think the first automobiles compare to modern cars?

    Sample: Modern cars are faster, safer, more comfortable.
3. Debate: Advantages vs. Disadvantages

Divide the class into two groups: one side discussing the advantages of the first automobiles, the other discussing disadvantages.

Sample: Speed closer to trains than horses eventually, no animal waste.
Sample: Pollution, noise, accidents, expensive.
Reflection

Ask students: "What do you think was the most significant impact of the first automobiles?"

Sample: The most significant impact was likely the redesign of cities.
